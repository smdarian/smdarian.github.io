I study the role of politics of data toward advocacy and political progress by nonprofit organizations and their clients. Specifically, I research how asylum seekers and organizations consider, navigate and use information technology at the border. I conduct qualitative, data science, and community based research methods to contribute formative requirements for system design. My primary research fields are Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Computer-Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW).
